Thank you for your careful reading of my manuscript and for the unusually detailed feedback. Most desk rejections offer little beyond a standard paragraph, and I genuinely appreciate the time you took to articulate your reasons.

Your criticisms are well taken. On reflection, the manuscript did try to cover too much ground — a four-level phylogenetic hierarchy spanning bacteria to humans — without giving any single level the depth it deserved. The result, as you noted, read more like a programmatic sketch than a fully argued academic article. I also take seriously your concern about AI-assisted writing. The version you received was copy-edited with language-model assistance, and I can see how the prose lost the texture of individual authorial voice in the process.

I have since substantially revised the manuscript. The new version narrows its scope to the human-specific microbiome–macrobiome dynamic tension, develops the theoretical framework more rigorously, removes the phylogenetic hierarchy (retaining only a brief evolutionary context), and was written without AI assistance at the prose level. It is now closer to what a focused journal article should be, rather than the book-length treatment the topic might ultimately deserve.

Whether the revised version finds a home remains to be seen, but your feedback was instrumental in prompting the revision. Thank you again for your time and candour.
