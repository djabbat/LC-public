Afaf,

I've just read an important new paper in *Frontiers in Neuroscience* (published 3 June 2026):

**Gou, Y., Liu, X., Zhu, W., Yuan, Y., Wang, Y., and Xie, Q.** — "Beyond the 'second brain': the gut microbiota as a constitutive co-constructor of embodied cognitive network."

DOI: 10.3389/fnins.2026.1808839

The paper argues that the gut microbiota is a constitutive co-constructor of embodied cognition — not merely a modulator, but an integral part of the cognitive architecture itself. The evidence chain is solid: germ-free animal models, FMT studies, human multi-omics + neuroimaging, and clinical intervention trials. It's directly relevant to our HAP/NHAM direction.

I've written a critical commentary in response (attached). My main argument: the authors focus exclusively on the **microbiome** and completely ignore the **macrobiome** — social, linguistic, and cultural environment — which is demonstrably a far more fundamental constitutive contributor to human cognition. I also raise the concern that their framework implicitly reflects a materialist (Marxist) bias: "being determines consciousness" translated into microbiological terms.

But more importantly, I see a **concrete collaboration opportunity**.

The authors explicitly mention digital twin technology as a future direction. We are developing a **whole-body physical simulator** — an *in vitro* platform covering the oral cavity, urogenital system, small intestine, and large intestine — coupled with a digital twin. This is exactly what they need to validate their theoretical framework experimentally.

**Could you reach out to the authors** (corresponding authors: Qinglian Xie, xieqinglian123@wchscu.cn; Yu Wang, wangyu202208@gmail.com) and propose a collaboration?

1. We share their vision of microbiota as a deeply integrated biological system
2. We offer a unique **digital-physical simulation platform** that can test their constitutive hypothesis under controlled conditions
3. We propose joint development of a digital-physical twin of the human gut-brain axis
4. Our platform can measure real-time metabolite fluxes, microbial community dynamics, and simulated neural signaling across multiple gut compartments
5. This would accelerate the translation from theory to clinical applications (precision psychobiotics, targeted interventions)
