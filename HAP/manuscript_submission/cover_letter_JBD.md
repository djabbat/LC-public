Dear Editor,

We submit our manuscript entitled **«Hepato-Affective Dynamics: A Nonlinear Dynamical Systems Framework for Bifurcation-Driven Affective Transitions»** for consideration in the *Journal of Biological Dynamics*.

**Why Journal of Biological Dynamics?**

This manuscript presents a novel 6-variable nonlinear dynamical system that captures the feedback loops between hepatic steroid output, brain steroid sensitivity, affective circuit integrity, inflammation, HPA axis activity, and metabolic state. The core contribution is a **bifurcation-driven mechanism** for the emergence of affective states — the model exhibits a sharp saddle-node bifurcation at a critical hepatic steroid threshold (L_basal ≈ 0), providing the first mathematical expression of the necessary condition for affect across Bilateria.

This work directly aligns with JBD's mission at the intersection of dynamical systems theory and the life sciences. The manuscript contributes:
- A new biologically grounded ODE system with rich dynamical behaviour
- Rigorous bifurcation analysis identifying critical transitions
- Global sensitivity analysis (Morris + Sobol) quantifying parameter dominance
- Demonstration of structural stability under stochastic perturbation

**Key contributions:**

1. **6-variable ODE model** coupling hepatic, neural, inflammatory, endocrine, and metabolic dynamics
2. **Saddle-node bifurcation** at L_basal ≈ 0 — the critical hepatic threshold
3. **Global sensitivity analysis** (Morris + Sobol) — hepatic threshold parameter θ_L dominates (Sobol ST = 0.75), confirming hepatic parameters drive the system
4. **Bifurcation boundary mapping** — 2D scan (L_basal × θ_L, 900 simulations) delineating the affect/no-affect phase boundary
5. **Stochastic robustness** — white noise CV = 0.78%, Ornstein-Uhlenbeck coloured noise CV = 1.59%
6. **In silico ablation experiments** reproducing the central biological prediction: hepatic ablation before critical window → A ≈ 0

**Interdisciplinary relevance:**

The model bridges nonlinear dynamics with comparative biology and clinical medicine. It generates specific, falsifiable predictions for conditional knockout experiments (zebrafish), pharmacological interventions (FXR/TGR5 agonists), and clinical biomarker studies (NAFLD-depression comorbidity, meta-analysis N = 2,126,593).

**Novelty:** To our knowledge, this is the first application of bifurcation theory to the phylogenetic emergence of affective states, and the first mathematical model to position the liver — not the brain — as the primary dynamical driver of affect.

**Prior publication:** The underlying theory was published as Tqemaladze, J. (2026) DOI: 10.65649/d76f6c48. An earlier preprint explored an initial formulation (El Fettahi & Tqemaladze, 2025, Preprints.org); the co-author withdrew in June 2026. The present manuscript is a sole-author, substantially revised version with new sensitivity analysis, stochastic robustness testing, 2D parameter scanning, and extended clinical validation not present in any prior version.
