Dear Editor,

We submit our manuscript entitled **«Hepato-Affective Primacy: A Nonlinear Dynamical Systems Framework»** for consideration in *Psychoneuroendocrinology*.

**Why Psychoneuroendocrinology?**

Our manuscript proposes that hepatic steroids — bile acids in vertebrates, ecdysteroids in arthropods — are the *primary* endocrine signals enabling affective states in Bilateria. This is a direct contribution to the journal's core mission: understanding how endocrine systems interface with brain and behaviour. The model we present formalises the liver as the central steroid-producing organ of affect, with FXR/TGR5 nuclear receptors in affective circuits as the molecular targets — a framework that integrates hepatic endocrinology, neurosteroid signalling, and computational psychiatry under one dynamical system.

**Key contributions:**

1. **A 6-variable ODE model** coupling hepatic steroid output (L), brain steroid sensitivity (B), affective circuit integrity (A), inflammation (I), HPA axis (S), and metabolism (M)
2. **In silico ablation experiments** reproducing the central prediction: hepatic ablation before critical developmental window → A ≈ 0
3. **Bifurcation analysis** — saddle-node bifurcation at L_basal ≈ 0, the mathematical expression of the necessary condition
4. **Morris/Sobol global sensitivity analysis** — hepatic threshold parameter θ_L dominates (Sobol ST = 0.75)
5. **Stochastic robustness** — white noise CV = 0.78%, Ornstein-Uhlenbeck colored noise CV = 1.59%
6. **2D parameter scan** (L_basal × θ_L, 900 simulations) mapping the affect/no-affect bifurcation boundary
7. **Clinical validation** against: bile acid dysregulation in MDD (PMID: 39719433, 40362260), NAFLD-depression comorbidity (PMID: 38689730, meta-analysis N = 2,126,593), Mendelian randomisation (PMID: 39227758, OR = 1.016), and post-transplant mood outcomes (PMID: 41763937)
8. **Experimental evidence:** FXR modulation restores bile acid homeostasis and produces antidepressant effects in rats (PMID: 39489929); TGR5/FXR knockout causes behavioural abnormalities (PMID: 39566821); bile acids directly modulate neuroinflammation via NF-κB suppression (PMID: 41459016)
9. **Five falsifiable predictions:** zebrafish conditional hepatocyte knockout, FXR/TGR5 pharmacology, bile acid biomarker panel for MDD, neonatal inflammation → adult affect, comparative invertebrate predictions

**Relevance to Psychoneuroendocrinology:**

The journal has a distinguished history of publishing work at the interface of endocrine signalling and affective neuroscience. Our manuscript extends this tradition in a novel direction: identifying the *liver* — not the HPA axis, not the gonads — as the phylogenetically primary endocrine organ of affect. The model provides a formal framework for investigating how hepatic steroid dynamics shape affective circuit development and maintenance, with direct implications for understanding the endocrine basis of depression, anxiety, and stress-related disorders.

**Novelty:** To our knowledge, this is the first mathematical model to propose the liver as the primary endocrine organ of affect, unifying comparative endocrinology, clinical psychoneuroendocrinology, and computational neuroscience.

**Prior publication:** The HAP theory was published as Tqemaladze, J. (2026) DOI: 10.65649/d76f6c48. An earlier preprint explored an initial formulation (El Fettahi & Tqemaladze, 2025, Preprints.org); the co-author withdrew in June 2026, and the present manuscript is a sole-author, substantially revised version (new sensitivity analysis, stochastic robustness, 2D parameter scan, alternative model formulations, extended clinical validation).
