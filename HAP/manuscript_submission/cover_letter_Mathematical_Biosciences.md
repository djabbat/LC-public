Dear Editors,

I am submitting a Research Article entitled **"Hepato-Affective Dynamics: A Nonlinear Dynamical Systems Framework for the Hepato-Affective Primacy (HAP) Theory"** for consideration in *Mathematical Biosciences*.

**Why Mathematical Biosciences?** The manuscript presents a 6-variable nonlinear ODE system with bifurcation analysis, global sensitivity analysis (Morris & Sobol), and stochastic robustness testing — applied to a novel biological question: how hepatic steroid output enables affective circuit development. The journal's scope — mathematical modelling of biological systems — is an exact match.

**Novelty.** This is the first mathematical formalisation of the Hepato-Affective Primacy (HAP) Theory (Tqemaladze, 2026), which established that a functional hepatic organ is a necessary condition for affective states across 56 bilaterian taxa (p < 0.0001). The model:
1. Derives a saddle-node bifurcation at the hepatic threshold (L_basal ≈ 0) — the mathematical expression of HAP's central claim
2. Identifies hepatic parameters as dominant via Sobol sensitivity analysis (ST = 0.75 for θ_L)
3. Validates qualitative predictions against three independent clinical datasets (NAFLD-depression meta-analysis, bile acid profiles in MDD, liver transplant mood outcomes)
4. Generates falsifiable predictions for zebrafish conditional knockout and FXR/TGR5 pharmacology

**Prior submissions.** The manuscript has been declined by 7 journals (J. Affective Disorders, Medical Hypotheses, Psychoneuroendocrinology, BioSystems, BSPC, Chaos Solitons & Fractals, Bulletin of Mathematical Biology). These were desk rejects — the manuscript has not undergone peer review. Feedback consistently indicated scope mismatch rather than methodological flaws. Mathematical Biosciences is the natural home: it publishes exactly this type of work — nonlinear dynamical models of biological systems.
