About the Alliance

Building rigorous scientific infrastructure for longevity research in Georgia and beyond.

Who We Are

Georgia Longevity Alliance (GLA) is a registered non-governmental scientific organization based in Tbilisi, Georgia. We were established to create high-quality, internationally visible scientific publishing infrastructure for longevity and aging research — a field of growing global significance that lacked dedicated venues in the South Caucasus region.

We operate Annals of Rejuvenation Science, a peer-reviewed open-access journal covering empirical intervention studies, biomarker validation, and translational research in aging and longevity medicine.

GLA is a member of the global Longevity Alliance network and operates in partnership with Longevity Clinic, Inc. (Resort Abastumani, Georgia) and the Ilia Vekua Young Scientists Club.

Mission & Vision

Mission

To advance mechanistic, causal, and translational research on biological aging through rigorous open-access scientific publishing, structured peer review, and support for early-career researchers.

Vision

A world in which the biological mechanisms of aging are fully understood and can be safely and equitably addressed — extending healthy human lifespan through evidence-based intervention.

Editorial Philosophy

Our journals explicitly prioritize mechanistic, causal research over phenomenological description, and theory-testing experiments over exploratory surveys. We welcome:

  • Negative results with adequate statistical power
  • Pre-registered replication studies
  • Adversarial tests of existing theoretical frameworks
  • Quantitative models with explicit, falsifiable predictions
  • Methodological critiques and statistical re-analyses

All articles undergo single-blind peer review by independent domain experts and are published under the CC BY 4.0 license with permanent CrossRef DOI assignment.
