About the Organization

Georgia Longevity Alliance is a registered non-governmental organization (Reg. № 404506520, Georgia) dedicated to advancing the scientific study of biological aging. We operate peer-reviewed open-access publishing infrastructure and coordinate one of the largest peer review networks in Georgian biomedical science.

We are a member of the global Longevity Alliance network, connecting us to longevity-focused organizations and researchers worldwide.

Our work creates durable, open infrastructure — published science, indexed metadata, trained reviewers — that compounds in value over time and benefits the entire longevity research community.

Funding Priorities

Grant support would allow us to expand our scientific impact in several concrete directions:

International Indexing

Applying to PubMed Central, Scopus, and DOAJ to increase discoverability and credibility of published research.

Research Grants Program

Small grants for early-career Georgian researchers to conduct original aging and longevity science studies.

Open Science Infrastructure

Building preprint servers, open data repositories, and statistical review capacity to meet international publishing standards.

Scientific Events

Annual longevity science symposium bringing together Georgian and international researchers, clinicians, and policymakers.

Why Longevity Research in Georgia?

Georgia is underrepresented in global longevity science despite having a well-developed university system and strong medical tradition. Supporting GLA creates high-leverage scientific capacity in an under-resourced region, generates open-access research freely available worldwide, and contributes to building a sustainable regional research ecosystem.
