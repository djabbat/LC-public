Why MCOA exists

The geroscience field has, for two decades, oscillated between single-driver theories of aging — telomere attrition, epigenetic clocks, mitochondrial dysfunction, proteostasis collapse, senescence — each accompanied by strong evidence in some tissues and embarrassing exceptions in others. Pure-driver theories cannot simultaneously explain why centenarian heart tissue can resemble that of a 60-year-old while their hematopoietic stem-cell compartment shows full age-matched exhaustion, or why caloric restriction lengthens lifespan in mice but barely budges it in rhesus macaques on similar protocols.

MCOA proposes that aging is not a driver but the weighted sum of several independent counters, with weights that differ between tissues. The framework is deliberately spare: it does not specify which counters exist; it specifies the conditions under which a given counter set is falsified. This is a meta-theory, not a candidate theory — it is the scaffolding into which CDATA, telomere theories, epigenetic clocks, and proteostatic theories all plug as concrete instantiations.

Core formalism

Tissue-level damage L at organism age n (number of cell divisions in the lineage) and chronological time t:

L_tissue(n, t) = Σᵢ wᵢ(tissue) · fᵢ(Dᵢ(n, t))

The damage state of counter i in that tissue:

Dᵢ(n, t) = Dᵢ₀ + αᵢ·(n/nᵢ*) + βᵢ·(t/τᵢ) + γᵢ·I(other counters)

where αᵢ is the division-linked damage rate (per Hayflick-equivalent), βᵢ is the time-linked rate (per characteristic time τᵢ), and γᵢ·I(other counters) captures cross-coupling — for example, mitochondrial ROS accelerating telomere oxidation. wᵢ(tissue) encodes that the same counter contributes very differently in proliferative vs. post-mitotic tissues.

fᵢ(·) is a saturating non-linearity (default sigmoid) that prevents the model from extrapolating linearly past biologically realistic damage levels.

Inviolable axioms

The framework is bound by four axioms. Any extension that violates them is, by definition, no longer MCOA.

M1 — Parallel counters. Aging is driven by ≥ 2 distinct damage-accumulation processes proceeding in parallel. No single counter is sufficient. (A model with one counter is excluded; a model with five counters of which only one carries weight in every tissue is also excluded — the constraint applies tissue-by-tissue.)
M2 — Dimensional consistency. No expression α·n + β·t is valid unless reduced to common dimensionless form. Counters live on the unit interval [0, 1]; time and division counts are normalised by their respective scales.
M3 — A-priori tissue weighting. Tissue weights wᵢ(tissue) must be predicted BEFORE fitting, from independent cell-biological parameters (proliferation index, mitochondrial density, transcriptional fidelity, etc.). Post-hoc fitting of weights to outcome data is prohibited — it would render the model unfalsifiable.
M4 — Falsifiability is first-class. Operational threshold (v5.6): MCOA is falsified if on a pre-registered cohort N ≥ 2000 at α = 0.001, partial r² for all-cause mortality (after age + sex adjustment) falls below 0.05 for every counter i. Power analysis: N=1875 yields 80 % power for R² = 0.30 detection. The All-of-Us release WG-3 (anticipated 2026 Q4, N≈4500 with deep biomarker phenotyping) is the canonical first test set.

Falsification protocol (Axiom M4 expanded)

Step 1 — A-priori weight assignment

For each candidate counter, derive wᵢ(tissue) from at least two cell-biological independent measurements (proliferation index from Ki-67 staining, mitochondrial density from citrate-synthase activity, etc.). Document the derivation in a public, time-stamped pre-registration before any outcome data is touched.

Step 2 — Outcome cohort

Pre-registered N ≥ 2000 cohort with deep multi-tissue phenotyping and ≥ 10-year mortality follow-up. All-of-Us WG-3 release is the canonical target.

Step 3 — Statistical test

Compute partial r² for all-cause mortality (Cox regression, age + sex covariates) per counter i. If every counter falls below the 0.05 threshold at α = 0.001 (Bonferroni-corrected for the number of counters tested), MCOA is rejected.

Step 4 — Asymmetry

The framework is rejected only if every counter fails. A single counter passing rescues the framework; this is by design — MCOA claims the existence of multiple counters, not the relevance of any specific one.

FAQ

Is this just "aging is multifactorial"?

No. "Aging is multifactorial" is a vague claim, not a model. MCOA gives a specific functional form (Σᵢ wᵢ · fᵢ(Dᵢ)), specific kinetic constraints (Axiom M2), specific protection against curve-fitting (Axiom M3), and a specific falsification threshold (Axiom M4). Each is operationalised in code.

How is this different from the GeroScience hallmarks?

The López-Otín hallmarks are a curated list with no integrative formalism and no falsification criterion. MCOA is a mathematical framework — counters can be the hallmarks, but they have to enter as quantified Dᵢ with kinetic parameters and a-priori weights, not as descriptive labels.

What does CDATA's "INCONCLUSIVE" status mean for MCOA?

Counter status is independent of framework status. CDATA being inconclusive means we do not yet know if centriolar polyglutamylation is a useful instance of MCOA. Telomere attrition and epigenetic drift are well-established counters; they alone are sufficient to keep the framework alive (Axiom M4 requires every counter to fail).
