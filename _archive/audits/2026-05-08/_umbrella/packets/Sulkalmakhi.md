## 1. Mission & charter alignment

Sulkalmakhi’s mission is to **catalyse local civic, ecological, and cultural development** in the Samtskhe-Javakheti region, with a focus on Abastumani and surrounding communities. The NGO operates strictly within the non-commercial framework defined by its charter:

- **Art. 1.6** – Purpose is non-commercial; auxiliary entrepreneurial activity allowed only to serve objectives; profit distribution to members/founders/officers **prohibited**.
- **Art. 1.7** – Asset alienation permitted if it serves the activity, organisational development, objectives, or charitable purposes.
- **Art. 1.8** – Right to undertake any activity not prohibited by law, even if not explicitly listed in the charter.

The two active sub-projects (ecological camp and cultural metalwork workshop) directly reflect this mission and are fully within the charter’s scope.

## 2. Strategic pillars (2026–2031)

### 2.1 Local ecology & community engagement (Abastumani / Adigeni)
- Restore and maintain the River Otskhe oasis (5 km stretch) as a clean, safe recreational zone.
- Involve local residents, visitors, and youth in hands‑on eco‑actions (cleanups, re‑vegetation, monitoring).
- Create a replicable model for small‑scale civic environmental management in mountain resorts.

### 2.2 Cultural heritage preservation (Georgian iconography metalwork)
- Establish the *Mamne the Goldsmith* („მამნე ოქრომჭედელი“) metalwork workshop in Atskuri community centre.
- Teach traditional Georgian *khatchedva* (icon‑metal chasing) techniques to youth and adults.
- Preserve and transmit a craft with over 2,500 surviving museum‑quality icons – a unique Georgian cultural asset.

### 2.3 Youth education & creative camps
- Run integrative summer camps combining environmental education, craft workshops, and creative play.
- Target groups: schoolchildren, teenagers, and young adults from Abastumani and surrounding villages.
- Use a project‑based learning model – participants are co‑creators of the eco‑oasis and workshop output.

### 2.4 Sustainable funding
- **Project grants** – target local (GEL), national (Georgian state, EU‑funded), and international donors for eco‑ and cultural initiatives.
- **Auxiliary entrepreneurial activity** (Art. 1.6) – e.g., sale of handmade metalwork, paid workshops, eco‑tourism services (profit stays in NGO).
- **Membership dues** (Art. 3.6(c)) – volunteer contributions from active members.
- **Donations** – direct support from individuals and businesses.

No profit distribution to any person (Art. 1.6).

## 3. Georgian NCJP legal-form implications

### 3.1 Taxation
- **Profit tax:** NCJPs are generally exempt from profit tax on income from non-commercial activities (Georgian Tax Code Art. 98). Auxiliary entrepreneurial income may be taxable if it exceeds GEL 500,000/year or is not directly related to the NGO's objectives.
- **VAT:** Registration required if annual turnover exceeds GEL 100,000 (Art. 103). Most grants and donations are VAT-exempt.
- **Property tax:** Exempt for property used directly for non-commercial purposes (Art. 148).
- **Withholding tax:** On salaries, dividends (if any), and payments to non-residents.

### 3.2 Audit requirements
- **Annual financial statements** must be submitted to NAPR by 31 March of the following year (Georgian Law on Entrepreneurs, Art. 17).
- **External audit** is mandatory if annual revenue exceeds GEL 1,000,000 or if the NGO receives public funding > GEL 500,000 (Law on Accounting, Art. 12).
- Sulkalmakhi's current budget (< $15,000) does not trigger mandatory audit, but best practice is to maintain auditable records.

### 3.3 Profit-distribution ban
- **Art. 1.6** explicitly prohibits distribution of profits to founders, members, or officers.
- **Art. 6.6** extends this to liquidation residue.
- Violation can lead to revocation of NCJP status and personal liability for directors (Georgian Civil Code Art. 52).

### 3.4 Auxiliary economic activity
- **Art. 1.6** allows auxiliary entrepreneurial activity, provided:
  - Profit is used exclusively for the NGO's objectives.
  - Activity does not become the primary purpose.
  - Activity is not prohibited by law.
- Examples: sale of handmade metalwork, paid workshops, eco-tourism services.
- Income from such activity must be reported separately in financial statements.

## 6. Mamne the Goldsmith heritage context

- **Technique:** Repoussé and chasing on silver, gold, or copper; often combined with enamel and niello.
- **Current state:** The craft is endangered – only a handful of master artisans remain in Georgia, mostly in Tbilisi and Kutaisi.
- **Atskuri location:** Atskuri (Adigeni municipality) has a historic fortress and a community centre that can host the workshop. The centre already runs other craft classes (pottery, weaving).

- **Community impact:** The river is a popular swimming and recreation spot. Cleanup would reduce health risks and enhance the resort's appeal.
