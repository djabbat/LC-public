Aging is associated with progressive decline in cardiac structure and function. 
Accumulating evidence in model organisms and humans links cardiac aging to 
mitochondrial regulation, encompassing a complex interplay of mitochondrial 
morphology, mitochondrial ROS, mitochondrial DNA mutations, mitochondrial 
unfolded protein response, nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide levels and 
sirtuins, as well as mitophagy. This review summarizes the recent discoveries on 
the mitochondrial regulation of cardiac aging and the possible molecular 
mechanisms underlying the anti-aging effects, as well as the potential 
interventions that alleviate aging-related cardiac diseases and attenuate 
cardiac aging via the regulation of mitochondria.
