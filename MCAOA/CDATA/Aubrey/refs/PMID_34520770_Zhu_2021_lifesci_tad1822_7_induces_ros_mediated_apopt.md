HER2-positive breast cancer (HER2-BC) shows the over-expression of tyrosine 
kinase receptor EphB4 associated with poor disease prognosis. E-cadherin is 
found as a survival factor in multiple models of breast cancer by suppressing 
reactive oxygen-mediated apoptosis. This study confirmed that both HER2 and 
EphB4 are positively correlated with E-cadherin in HER2-BC. Inhibition of HER2 
or EphB4 is discovered to induce ROS-dependent apoptosis by decreasing 
E-cadherin expression in SKBR3 and MDA-MB-453 cells. TAD1822-7 (TAD), a novel 
biphenyl urea taspine derivative, exhibits good growth inhibition, apoptosis 
induction and ROS accumulation effects on SKBR3 and MDA-MB-453 cells. 
Mechanistic investigation revealed that TAD blockades both EphB4 positive signal 
transduction and activation of HER2 signal transduction, thereby suppressing 
E-cadherin/TGF-β/p-Smad2/3 signaling axis to elicit ROS-dependent endogenous 
mitochondrial apoptosis. Together, these findings not only provide a new 
approach for HER2-BC therapy but also increase our understanding of the 
regulating effect of E-cadherin by HER2 and EphB4 in ROS-mediated apoptosis.
