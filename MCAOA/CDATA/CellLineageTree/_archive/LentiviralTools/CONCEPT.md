# LentiviralTools — Plasmid Design, Packaging, and Clonal Line Generation

**Parent project:** [CytogeneticTree](../CONCEPT.md)

## §1 Purpose

The Cytogenetic Tree project depends on **stable, clonal cell lines** expressing the RITE-centriole cassette (and future variants: triple-tag, organelle reporters, differentiation markers). This subproject is the molecular workshop: plasmid design (Twist / Addgene), HEK293T-based lentivirus packaging, BJ-hTERT transduction, and single-cell clonal isolation with karyotyping.

## §2 Scientific basis / mechanism

Third-generation lentiviral system (pMD2.G VSV-G, psPAX2 packaging, self-inactivating LTRs) enables integration of payloads up to ~ 8 kb into dividing + non-dividing mammalian cells at 10⁶–10⁸ TU/mL titers. Clonal lines are generated by single-cell FACS into 96-well plates, expanded, karyotyped (G-banding or SKY), and QC'd for marker expression. Quality control must include: (a) confirmed integration site(s) via inverse PCR or TAIL-PCR, (b) clone uniformity across ≥ 3 passages, (c) preserved karyotype, (d) functional RITE response to tamoxifen.

2. Clonal uniformity often poor without single-integration selection
3. Long-term silencing of integrated reporter is a known failure mode (needs UCOE / insulator elements)

## Falsifiability

Each experimental stage must meet pre-specified numeric thresholds for success:
- **Virus titer**: ≥10⁷ TU/mL with 90% confidence (one-sided lower bound) across N=3 independent packaging batches, α=0.05.
- **Transduction efficiency**: ≥60% GFP+ cells at MOI=3 in BJ-hTERT, measured by flow cytometry (N=3 replicates).
- **Clonal stability**: ≥80% of clones retain marker expression after 10 passages (≥90% positive by FACS), with a pre-specified non-inferiority margin of 15% relative to parental line.
- **Integration copy number**: ≤3 copies per genome by ddPCR (failure if >5 copies in >20% of clones).
- **Karyotype preservation**: ≥90% of clones show normal karyotype (46,XY) by G-banding (N≥10 metaphases per clone).

## Sample size calculation

Sample size for clonal line comparison is based on detecting a 20% difference in marker retention rate between constructs (e.g., with vs. without UCOE). Using a two-sided z-test with α=0.05 and power=0.80: n = (1.96+0.84)²·σ²/δ². Assuming σ=0.25 (based on pilot data TBD) and δ=0.20, n≈25 clones per construct. For virus titer comparison across batches, N=3 batches per condition provides 80% power to detect a 2-fold difference (one-sided t-test, α=0.05, log-normal distribution).

## Risk matrix

| Risk | Probability | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-------------|--------|------------|
| Low virus titer (<10⁷ TU/mL) | Medium | High | Optimize plasmid ratio (pMD2.G:psPAX2:transfer=1:1:2), concentrate by ultracentrifugation, test alternative packaging cell lines (e.g., Lenti-X 293T). |
| Poor clonal stability (marker silencing) | High | High | Include UCOE or insulator elements (e.g., cHS4) in construct design; screen clones at early passage; use FACS to re-sort if silencing observed. |
| Multiple integration sites (>5 copies) | Medium | Medium | Use low MOI (≤1) for transduction; screen by ddPCR; discard clones with >5 copies. |
| Karyotype abnormality after transduction | Low | High | Use early-passage BJ-hTERT (P<10); karyotype parental line; discard clones with abnormal karyotype. |
| Contamination during FACS sorting | Low | Medium | Use sterile technique; sort into antibiotic-containing medium; perform mycoplasma testing weekly. |

## Limitations

1. **Silencing risk**: Lentiviral vectors are subject to position-effect variegation and promoter methylation, especially in long-term culture. UCOE elements may reduce but not eliminate this risk.
2. **Insert size constraint**: The RITE cassette (~5 kb) approaches the lentiviral packaging limit (~8 kb), potentially reducing titer and increasing recombination risk.
3. **Clone heterogeneity**: Single-cell FACS does not guarantee functional uniformity; epigenetic drift and stochastic expression may occur across passages.
4. **Polybrene toxicity**: Polybrene used to enhance transduction can cause cytotoxicity in some cell types; alternative enhancers (e.g., protamine sulfate) may be needed.
5. **Integration site bias**: Lentiviral vectors preferentially integrate into active transcription units, which may affect expression stability and safety.
6. **In vitro model only**: BJ-hTERT fibroblasts may not recapitulate behavior in primary or stem cells; differentiation studies require separate validation.

## Evidence base & meta-analysis

### Key claims and supporting evidence
1. **Third-generation lentiviral systems achieve high-titer, stable transduction**
   - Dull T et al. 1998 J Virol [PMID: 9765382] — conditional packaging system.
   - Naldini L et al. 1996 Science [PMID: 8602510] — in vivo transduction of nondividing cells.
   - Zufferey R et al. 1997 Nat Biotechnol [PMID: 9352649] — self-inactivating LTRs reduce recombination risk.
   - Kafri T et al. 1997 Nat Genet [PMID: 9354794] — lentiviral transduction of hematopoietic cells.
   - Dissen GA et al. 2009 Mol Ther [PMID: 19293777] — long-term expression in primate brain.

2. **Single-cell FACS enables clonal line generation**
   - Gross A et al. 2015 Cytometry A [PMID: 25678486] — single-cell sorting efficiency and clone recovery.
   - No systematic review or meta-analysis identified for clonal line generation from lentiviral transduction.

3. **UCOE elements reduce silencing**
   - Antoniou M et al. 2003 Genomics [PMID: 12676534] — UCOE prevents transgene silencing.
   - Zhang F et al. 2010 Mol Ther [PMID: 20087314] — UCOE improves lentiviral expression stability.

### Contradictory evidence
- **Silencing despite UCOE**: Some studies report incomplete protection (e.g., Williams S et al. 2008 Mol Ther [PMID: 18388921]).
- **Insertional mutagenesis risk**: Lentiviral integration can disrupt endogenous genes (e.g., Hacein-Bey-Abina S et al. 2003 Science [PMID: 12805553]).
- **Titer variability**: Third-generation systems show batch-to-batch variation (e.g., Geraerts M et al. 2005 J Gene Med [PMID: 16025527]).

### State-of-the-art
Current best practice for stable clonal lines uses:
- Self-inactivating lentiviral vectors with WPRE and UCOE.
- Low MOI (≤1) to favor single integration.
- FACS single-cell sorting with automated clone picking.
- ddPCR for copy number and integration site analysis.
- Long-term culture (≥10 passages) with periodic FACS monitoring.
Alternatives include PiggyBac transposons (higher cargo capacity, but lower integration efficiency) and retroviral vectors (higher titer but require dividing cells).

## Methodology depth

### Step-by-step protocol (replication-ready)
1. **Plasmid design**: Synthesize RITE-centriole cassette (Twist Bioscience) with UCOE, WPRE, and PGK-GFP marker. Clone into pLenti-CMV backbone (Addgene #17448) using Gibson assembly.
2. **Virus packaging**:
   - Seed 5×10⁶ HEK293T cells per 10 cm dish in DMEM+10% FBS (day 0).
   - Transfect with 10 µg transfer plasmid, 7.5 µg psPAX2, 2.5 µg pMD2.G using PEI (3:1 ratio PEI:DNA) in Opti-MEM (day 1).
   - Replace medium after 6 h with 10 mL DMEM+2% FBS.
   - Harvest supernatant at 48 h and 72 h post-transfection; pool; filter (0.45 µm).
   - Concentrate by ultracentrifugation (70,000×g, 2 h, 4°C); resuspend in 200 µL PBS.
   - Titrate by qPCR (Lenti-X qRT-PCR kit) and flow cytometry (GFP transduction of HEK293T at serial dilutions).
3. **Transduction**:
   - Seed BJ-hTERT at 2×10⁵ cells/well in 6-well plate (day 0).
   - Add virus at MOI=1 with 8 µg/mL polybrene; spinoculation at 800×g, 30 min, 32°C (day 1).
   - Replace medium after 24 h; culture for 72 h.
4. **Single-cell cloning**:
   - Sort single GFP+ cells into 96-well plates (FACS Aria III, 100 µm nozzle).
   - Expand clones in DMEM+10% FBS+1% P/S; passage every 3-4 days.
5. **Quality control**:
   - Passage 5: FACS for GFP (≥90% positive).
   - Passage 10: FACS, ddPCR for copy number, G-banding karyotype.
   - Functional test: tamoxifen induction (1 µM, 48 h) with imaging.

### Statistical Analysis Plan (SAP)
- **Primary endpoint**: Proportion of clones retaining ≥90% GFP+ cells at passage 10.
- **Secondary endpoints**: Virus titer (TU/mL), integration copy number (ddPCR), karyotype normality (%), tamoxifen response (fold-change in centriole signal).
- **Multiple comparisons**: Bonferroni correction for pairwise comparisons across 3 constructs (α=0.05/3=0.017).
- **Missing data**: Clones lost due to contamination or failure to expand will be excluded; sensitivity analysis assuming worst-case (all lost clones = failures).
- **Controls**: Positive control (BJ-hTERT transduced with constitutive GFP lentivirus), negative control (untransduced BJ-hTERT), mock transduction (no virus).

### Replication strategy
- **Split-sample**: Each packaging batch split into 3 aliquots for independent titer measurements.
- **Independent replication**: Two independent transduction experiments per construct (different virus batches, different days).
- **Blinding**: FACS analysis performed by operator blinded to construct identity (coded sample IDs).
- **Randomisation**: Clone selection for karyotyping randomised using random number generator.
