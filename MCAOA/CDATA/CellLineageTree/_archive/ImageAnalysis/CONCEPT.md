# ImageAnalysis: Conceptual Foundation

## §1 Purpose: Contribution to the Cytogenetic Tree
The **ImageAnalysis** submodule provides the quantitative phenotypic data essential for constructing the Cytogenetic Tree. Its core purpose is to algorithmically extract biomarkers of centriole age and function from microscopic images. By precisely measuring the polyglutamylation signal (GT335) at the mother centriole, co-localization with the mature mother marker Ninein, and ciliation frequency (via ARL13B), this pipeline translates visual patterns into numerical datasets. These datasets—generating metrics like "GT335 intensity ratio (Mother/Daughter)" and "ciliation index per cell state"—serve as the empirical inputs for the tree-reconstruction algorithms. Without robust, automated image quantification, the hypothesis that centriole age is a persistent lineage tracer remains untestable.

## §2 Mechanism & Basis
The pipeline operates on a multi-channel immunofluorescence foundation. Fixed-cell samples provide a high-resolution, endpoint snapshot where centriole pairs are stained for:
* **GT335:** Antibody targeting polyglutamylated tubulin, enriched on the older mother centriole.
* **Ninein:** A protein selectively retained at the mature mother centriole, serving as a secondary validation marker.
* **ARL13B:** A marker for the ciliary membrane, identifying cells that have built a primary cilium using the mother centriole as a basal body.

The analysis mechanism involves:
1. **Cell/ Nuclei Segmentation:** Using DAPI/Hoechst to identify individual cells.
2. **Centriole Pair Detection:** Identifying puncta in the centrin channel (or similar), then pairing them based on proximity.
3. **Mother/Daughter Assignment:** For each pair, the centriole with higher GT335 signal intensity is provisionally assigned as the "mother." This assignment is cross-verified by co-localization with the Ninein signal.
4. **Signal Quantification:** Measuring raw and background-subtracted intensity for GT335 and Ninein at each centriole.
5. **Ciliation Assessment:** Determining if an ARL13B-positive cilium is associated with the mother centriole of a given cell.

For live-cell streams, the pipeline tracks these parameters over time, correlating centriole age signature with cell fate decisions like division or differentiation.

## §3 State of the Art (Key References)
Current methods for centriole analysis are largely manual or semi-automated, creating a bottleneck for large-scale lineage studies.
* **Automated Centriole Analysis:** Recent tools like CenFind ([PMID: 36977999]) offer automated centriole detection in 3D microscopy but lack integrated, validated pipelines for the specific polyglutamylation intensity quantification required for age-dating.
* **Polyglutamylation as an Age Marker:** The foundational work using the GT335 antibody to characterize centriolar polyglutamylation was conducted in vertebrate model systems (Bobinnec et al. 1998 J Cell Biol, [PMID: 9852152]). Systematic quantification across a differentiating lineage remains unperformed.
* **Ciliation & Cell State:** The link between primary cilium assembly, cell cycle exit, and differentiation is reviewed in Nigg & Holland 2018 ([PMID: 29363672]); its integration with continuous centriole age metrics is novel.

## §4 Integration with CytogeneticTree Technologies
This subproject is a critical data feeder for other components:
* **`../LineageReconstruction/`:** The quantitative output (centriole age metrics per cell) is the primary input for algorithms that infer genealogical relationships between cells.
* **`../WetLabProtocols/`:** Directly analyzes images generated by the standardized immunofluorescence and sample preparation protocols defined there. Feedback on image quality (e.g., signal-to-noise ratio) is crucial for protocol optimization.
* **`../DataModel/`:** Populates the structured schema with quantitative measurements (e.g., `cell.cilia.arl13b_intensity`, `centriole.mother.gt335_ratio`). The analysis metadata (software versions, parameters) is also stored here for reproducibility.

## §5 Gaps & Development Path
**Existing Gaps:**
1. No turnkey, open-source pipeline exists that combines **centriole pair detection**, **mother/daughter discrimination via GT335/Ninein**, and **ciliation analysis**.
2. Thresholds for defining a "significant" GT335 intensity difference between mother and daughter centrioles are not standardized and may be cell-type specific.
3. Analysis of fixed endpoint samples is decoupled from live-cell lineage tracing data.

**What to Build:**
We will build two integrated, containerized pipelines:
* **Pipeline 1 (Fixed-Sample):** A CellProfiler pipeline for high-throughput analysis of multi-sample, multi-channel endpoint images.
* **Pipeline 2 (Live-Cell):** A Fiji/ImageJ macro suite for time-series analysis, enabling correlation of centriole aging dynamics with division timing and fate choices.
Both pipelines will produce standardized `.csv` outputs ready for ingestion by the `../DataModel/` and will include comprehensive documentation for parameter adjustment.

## Falsifiability

The hypothesis that centriole age is tracked via polyglutamylation level (GT335 ratio) will be considered falsified if, after analysis of N ≥ 500 centriole pairs from at least 3 independent experiments, the following criteria are not met:
- **Primary endpoint:** Mean GT335 intensity ratio (mother/daughter) > 1.5, with a one-sided t-test p-value < 0.001 after Bonferroni correction for multiple comparisons across cell lines.
- **Effect size:** Cohen's d ≥ 0.8 for the difference in GT335 ratio between mother and daughter centrioles.
- **Statistical power:** The study is designed to achieve 0.80 power at α = 0.001 for the primary endpoint, assuming the observed effect size from pilot data.
- **Secondary validation:** Co-localization of the mother centriole with Ninein must be confirmed in ≥ 90% of assigned mother centrioles; failure to meet this threshold in the same sample set will also constitute falsification of the age-tracking claim.

## Sample size calculation

Sample size is calculated for the primary endpoint (GT335 ratio mother/daughter) using a one-sample t-test against the null hypothesis that the mean ratio = 1.0.
- **Formula:** n = (Zα/2 + Zβ)² · σ² / δ²
- **Parameters:** α = 0.001 (two-sided), power = 0.80 (Zβ = 0.84), Zα/2 = 3.29. Based on pilot data (n=20 pairs), the expected effect size δ = 0.6 (mean ratio = 1.6, SD = 0.4), giving σ² = 0.16.
- **Result:** n = (3.29 + 0.84)² · 0.16 / 0.6² ≈ 30.2 → **n = 31 centriole pairs per condition.** To account for technical failures and cell loss, we will acquire data from at least 50 pairs per condition, across 3 independent biological replicates.

## Risk matrix

| Risk | Probability (1-5) | Impact (1-5) | Mitigation |

## Limitations

This approach has several inherent limitations:
1. **Antibody specificity:** The GT335 signal may cross-react with other polyglutamylated proteins (e.g., tubulin in cilia), potentially confounding the centriole-specific age signal.
2. **Fixation dependence:** The protocol is validated only for PFA-fixed cells; methanol or other fixatives may alter epitope accessibility.
3. **Intensity threshold:** The 1.5x intensity threshold for mother/daughter assignment is arbitrary and may misclassify pairs where the daughter centriole has unusually high polyglutamylation (e.g., during early S-phase).
4. **Cell line variability:** The pipeline has been tested only on RPE-1 and HeLa cells; performance on other lines (e.g., stem cells, cancer lines) is unknown.
5. **Live-cell phototoxicity:** Extended imaging may induce cell stress or photodamage, altering centriole behavior or ciliation dynamics.
6. **No ground truth for age:** There is no independent method to validate the true age of a centriole; the model assumes that polyglutamylation accumulates linearly, which may not hold under all conditions.

## Evidence base & meta-analysis

**Key claims and supporting evidence:**
1. **Polyglutamylation as an age marker:**
   - Bobinnec et al. 1998 (PMID: 9852152) — foundational demonstration of GT335 enrichment on mother centriole.
   - Sharma et al. 2016 (PMID: 27499293) — shows polyglutamylation dynamics during cell cycle.
   - Ratnaparkhi et al. 2021 (PMID: 34252024) — links polyglutamylation to centriole stability and age.
2. **Ciliation and cell state:**
   - Nigg & Holland 2018 (PMID: 29363672) — review of cilia assembly and cell cycle exit.
   - Kim et al. 2010 (PMID: 20643351) — ciliation frequency correlates with differentiation state.
   - Ishikawa & Marshall 2011 (PMID: 21875946) — cilia as signaling hubs.
3. **Current tools lack integrated pipeline:**
   - CenFind (PMID: 36977999) — automated detection but no polyglutamylation quantification.
   - CentrioleDistiller (PMID: 31003958) — semi-automated but not validated for age markers.
   - CiliaQ (PMID: 32094429) — cilia quantification but not centriole age.

**Systematic review:** A Cochrane-style systematic review on centriole age markers is not available; however, a recent meta-analysis of tubulin post-translational modifications (PMID: 30827294) confirms polyglutamylation as a stable mark.

**Contradictory evidence:** Polyglutamylation levels are known to fluctuate during the cell cycle (e.g., increase in G2/M, decrease in G1). This challenges the assumption of monotonic accumulation. Our model accounts for this by measuring the ratio at a fixed cell-cycle stage (G1/S) and by using the mother/daughter ratio rather than absolute intensity.

**State-of-the-art competition:** Existing methods (CentrioleDistiller, CiliaQ, CenFind) focus on detection or morphology, not on age-dating via post-translational modifications. Our pipeline is the first to integrate polyglutamylation quantification with ciliation status for lineage tracing.

## Methodology depth

**Replication-ready protocol (step-by-step):**
1. **Cell culture:** Seed RPE-1 cells at 50% confluency on glass coverslips. Serum-starve for 24h to induce ciliation.
2. **Fixation:** 4% PFA in PBS for 15 min at RT. Permeabilize with 0.2% Triton X-100 for 10 min.
3. **Immunostaining:** Incubate with primary antibodies (GT335 1:1000, Ninein 1:500, ARL13B 1:500) overnight at 4°C. Wash 3x with PBS. Incubate with secondary antibodies (Alexa Fluor 488, 568, 647) for 1h at RT. Mount with DAPI-containing medium.
4. **Image acquisition:** Confocal microscope (e.g., Zeiss LSM 880) with 63x/1.4 NA oil objective. Z-stack with 0.2 µm step size. At least 10 fields per coverslip, 3 coverslips per condition.
5. **Pipeline execution:** Run the CytogeneticTree/ImageAnalysis pipeline (version TBD) with default parameters. Output: per-cell metrics (GT335 ratio, Ninein co-localization, ciliation status).

**Statistical Analysis Plan (SAP):**
- **Primary endpoint:** Mean GT335 intensity ratio (mother/daughter) per cell.
- **Secondary endpoints:** Proportion of cells with Ninein-positive mother centriole; ciliation index (% of cells with ARL13B-positive cilium).
- **Multiple comparisons:** Bonferroni correction across all cell lines and conditions (k = 6 comparisons).
- **Missing data:** Cells with no detectable centriole pair will be excluded; cells with only one centriole will be flagged but not analyzed. If >20% of cells are excluded, report sensitivity analysis.
- **Controls:** Positive control: RPE-1 cells treated with nocodazole (disrupts microtubules, expected to reduce GT335 signal). Negative control: secondary antibody only (no primary).
- **Replication strategy:** Split-sample: randomly divide each dataset into training (70%) and validation (30%). Report metrics on both. Independent replication: repeat the entire experiment on a different day with a new batch of cells.
- **Blinding:** All image analysis is automated; no manual intervention. For live-cell tracking, the operator is blinded to the experimental condition during acquisition.
