An optogenetic Bax has been designed that facilitates light-induced apoptosis. 
We demonstrate that mitochondrial recruitment of a genetically encoded 
light-responsive Bax results in the release of mitochondrial proteins, 
downstream caspase-3 cleavage, changes in cellular morphology, and ultimately 
cell death. Mutagenesis of a key phosphorylatable residue or modification of the 
C-terminus mitigates background (dark) levels of apoptosis that result from Bax 
overexpression. The mechanism of optogenetic Bax-mediated apoptosis was explored 
using a series of small molecules known to interfere with various steps in 
programmed cell death. Optogenetic Bax appears to form a mitochondrial 
apoptosis-induced channel analogous to that of endogenous Bax.
