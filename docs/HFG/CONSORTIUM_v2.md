## 7. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY (IP)

7.1 The IP regime shall be governed by the Horizon Europe MGA (Article 16, Annex 5, and the Specific Conditions).
7.2 Background IP remains the sole property of the providing Party.
7.3 Foreground IP generated by one Party alone will be owned by that Party. Jointly generated Foreground IP will be jointly owned by the generating Parties in equal shares, unless otherwise agreed in writing prior to the commencement of the relevant task.
7.4 Parties shall conclude a separate Joint Ownership Agreement for any Joint Foreground no later than six months after its creation, detailing management, exploitation, and royalty-sharing.

---

## 8. DATA SHARING AND PUBLICATION

8.1 All Beneficiaries have the right to publish Project results, subject to the provisions of the MGA (Article 17) and the following.
8.2 Any publication or dissemination activity must be submitted to the Consortium Board for review at least 30 calendar days prior to submission to ensure:
 a) Protection of Foreground IP.
 b) Compliance with confidentiality obligations.
 c) Accuracy and proper acknowledgment of all Consortium contributions.
8.3 A Party may request a delay of publication for up to 90 days to secure IP protection.
8.4 Clinical and epidemiological data sharing will comply with the approved IRB protocols, GDPR, and the Data Management Plan (DMP) of the Project.

---

## 9. GOVERNANCE AND DECISION-MAKING

**9.1 Consortium Board (CB):**
 * The CB is the main decision-making body and consists of one authorised representative from each Beneficiary plus the Scientific PI.
 * The Coordinator's representative chairs the CB.
 * CB meetings shall be held quarterly, either physically or virtually.

**9.2 Voting and Decision-Making:**
 * **Routine Operational Matters:** Decisions require a simple majority of votes (each Beneficiary has one vote; the PI has a vote only if formally representing a hosting Beneficiary).
 * **Matters Requiring Unanimity:** The following require the unanimous agreement of all Beneficiaries:
 * Amendment of this CA or the Project's scientific/technical objectives.
 * Any reallocation of the total budget exceeding 10% of a Beneficiary's allocation.
 * Decisions concerning the ownership, access rights, or exploitation of Foreground IP.
 * Admission or replacement of a Beneficiary.
 * **Family Relationship Recusal Protocol:** In any decision specifically concerning the budget, salary, or direct resource allocation for **Ms. Megi Sajaia (B1)** or **Prof. Otar Chokoshvili (B2)**, the affected spouse shall recuse themselves from the discussion and voting. The other Beneficiary representatives (and PI, if applicable) shall make the decision.

## 10. LIABILITY AND INDEMNIFICATION

10.1 Each Party's liability towards the other Parties is limited to the amount of funding it receives under the Project, except for cases of wilful misconduct, gross negligence, or breaches of confidentiality/IPR.
10.2 No Party shall be liable for any indirect or consequential damages suffered by another Party.

---

## 11. CONFIDENTIALITY

11.1 Parties agree to keep confidential all non-public information (technical, financial, administrative) exchanged under this CA for a period of five (5) years after the Project ends.
11.2 This obligation does not apply to information already publicly known, independently developed, or rightfully received from a third party without obligation of confidentiality.

---

## 12. TERMINATION AND EXIT PROCEDURES

12.1 A Party may voluntarily withdraw from the Consortium by giving three months' written notice to the Coordinator and CB, providing a plan for the handover of its tasks.
12.2 A Party may be expelled by unanimous decision of the other CB members for material breach of this CA or the GA, subject to a 30-day cure period.
12.3 Consequences of termination/exit will follow the rules of the Horizon Europe MGA (Article 18). The exiting Party shall transfer all relevant Foreground and provide necessary access to Background to ensure Project continuation.

---

## 13. CONFLICT RESOLUTION AND APPLICABLE LAW

13.1 The Parties shall seek to resolve any dispute arising from this CA amicably through good-faith negotiation, escalating to the Consortium Board if necessary.
13.2 If a dispute cannot be resolved amicably within 60 days, it shall be finally settled under the laws of **Georgia** by the competent courts of **Tbilisi, Georgia**.

---

## 14. DURATION AND MISCELLANEOUS

14.1 This CA enters into force upon signature by all Parties and shall remain effective for the duration of the Project (**36 months**) plus an additional **five (5) years** for post-project audit purposes, IP finalisation, and confidentiality obligations.
14.2 This CA, together with the signed Grant Agreement and its Annexes, constitutes the entire understanding between the Parties.
14.3 Amendments require a written addendum signed by all Parties.
14.4 Notices under this CA shall be in writing and sent to the contact details in Section 1.
