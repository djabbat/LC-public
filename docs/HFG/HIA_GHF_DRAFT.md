**2. PROJECT DESCRIPTION**

**2.1** This Agreement governs the relationship between the Parties concerning the Project **“LongevityCommon — Integrated Longevity Research Platform”** (the “Project”).

**2.2** The Project has been submitted for funding under the **EIC Pathfinder Open 2026** call (submission deadline 12 May 2026). The proposed duration is 36 months with a total requested grant of approximately €3,000,000 (three million Euros).

**2.3** The core scientific objective of the Project is to establish an integrated platform for longevity research. The work is structured into five Work Packages (WPs):
* **WP1:** Project Coordination & Data Governance Framework.
* **WP2:** Multi-Omics Biobank Development & Integration.
* **WP3:** AI-Driven Predictive Model Development for Age-Related Disease.
* **WP4:** Validation through Clinical & Pre-Clinical Studies.
* **WP5:** Dissemination, Exploitation, and Stakeholder Engagement.

**3. HOST INSTITUTION COMMITMENTS**

The Host hereby commits to:

**3.1** Act as the sole legal entity responsible towards the European Commission (EC) for the Grant Agreement, assuming all related administrative, legal, and financial responsibilities.

**3.2** Provide the PI with adequate administrative support and a dedicated physical or virtual workspace, including necessary IT infrastructure, to conduct the Project.

**3.3** Open and maintain a segregated bank account exclusively for the receipt and management of all Project funds from the EC.

**3.4** Establish and maintain a segregated, transparent ledger for all Project income and expenditures, in accordance with Horizon Europe financial rules.

**3.5** Be responsible for all accounting, fiscal obligations (including VAT and taxation), and statutory reporting related to the Project in Georgia.

**3.6** Submit all formal Project reports (periodic and final), including financial statements, to the EC in a timely manner, incorporating scientific and technical contributions prepared by the PI.

**3.7** Appoint an EC-approved independent accounting firm to conduct the mandatory annual audit of the Project’s financial statements.

**4. PRINCIPAL INVESTIGATOR COMMITMENTS**

The PI hereby commits to:

**4.1** Provide exclusive scientific leadership for the Project, ensuring the achievement of its objectives and the timely delivery of all scientific and technical deliverables.

**4.2** Devote a minimum of 50% of his full-time equivalent (FTE) working capacity to the Project throughout its duration.

**4.3** Prepare all scientific and technical content required for the Project reports to be submitted by the Host to the EC.

**4.4** Ensure the open science and timely publication of Project results in peer-reviewed journals and at international conferences, in compliance with Horizon Europe policies.

**4.5** Collaborate fully with the Host’s administrative staff to facilitate financial reporting and audit processes.

**5. FINANCIAL MANAGEMENT**

**5.1** All Project funds will be managed through the segregated bank account and ledger established by the Host (Clause 3.3 & 3.4).

**5.2** The PI’s salary, to be paid by the Host from the Project grant, will be calculated according to the Horizon Europe personnel cost rules, applying the relevant country correction coefficient for Georgia.

**5.3** All costs will be eligible, incurred, and reported in strict compliance with the EC Grant Agreement and the Horizon Europe Annotated Model Grant Agreement (HE MGA).

**5.4** The Host shall ensure the annual audit is performed by an accredited firm as per Clause 3.7.

**6. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY RIGHTS**

**6.1** The Parties agree that the ownership of results, including background and foreground intellectual property (IP), shall be governed by the default rules of the Horizon Europe MGA.

**6.2** Any foreground IP generated directly by the PI in the execution of the Project shall be jointly owned by the Host and the PI under the terms of the HE MGA. The Parties shall conclude a separate, more detailed agreement on the management and exploitation of joint foreground IP upon the successful signing of the Grant Agreement.

**7. DISPUTE RESOLUTION AND GOVERNING LAW**

**7.1** This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the substantive laws of Georgia.

**7.2** Any dispute arising out of or in connection with this Agreement shall be subject to an amicable settlement between the Parties. If no amicable settlement is reached within 60 (sixty) days, the dispute shall be finally settled by arbitration in Tbilisi, Georgia, under the rules of the Georgian International Arbitration Centre.

**8. TERMINATION**

**8.1** This Agreement may be terminated by mutual written consent of the Parties.

**8.2** Either Party may terminate this Agreement with immediate effect by written notice if the other Party is in material breach of its obligations and fails to remedy such breach within 30 days of receiving written notice.

**8.3** This Agreement will automatically terminate if the Project proposal is not selected for funding by the EC.

**8.4** Upon termination, the Host shall be responsible for fulfilling all remaining obligations to the EC. Rights and obligations under Clauses 6 (IP Rights), 7 (Dispute Resolution), and 9 (Validity) shall survive termination.

**9. VALIDITY**

This Agreement is valid from the date of its last signature and shall remain in force for the duration of the Project and for a period of 5 (five) years thereafter, primarily to cover audit, record-keeping, and IP management obligations.
